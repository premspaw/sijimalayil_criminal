Do not make any statement to the police without a lawyer present. Under Article 22 of the Constitution, you have the right to be informed of the grounds of arrest and to consult a lawyer of your choice. Call us immediately at +91 984 410 4360 — we handle emergency bail applications and can appear before the court at the earliest opportunity.
In most cases we can file a bail application within 24 hours of being briefed. For anticipatory bail matters — where arrest is anticipated but not yet made — we can file immediately. Our team is experienced in urgent bail hearings before Sessions Courts and the High Court of Karnataka.
Regular bail is applied for after arrest — it seeks release from custody. Anticipatory bail is applied for before arrest when a person apprehends arrest in connection with a non-bailable offence. Anticipatory bail under Section 438 CrPC is filed before the Sessions Court or High Court and, if granted, protects you from arrest.
Yes — NDPS is one of our primary criminal specialisations. NDPS cases involve strict bail conditions and harsh penalties, making experienced legal representation critical. We handle everything from bail applications (which require overcoming the reverse burden under Section 37 NDPS) through to full trial defence and High Court appeals.
PMLA cases involve the Enforcement Directorate investigating "proceeds of crime." The ED has wide powers of arrest, attachment of property, and search. PMLA carries a reverse burden of proof, making it essential to engage specialist lawyers early. We provide full PMLA defence including bail, challenging attachments, and trial representation.
